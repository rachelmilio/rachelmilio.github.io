The Attic Oratory Database is a project dedicated to showasing the corpus of Classical Athenian Oratory. This site catalogues the speeches of Attic Oratory, the events for which these speeches were written, and the people involved in these events and mentioned in these speeches. 

Navigate to the <a href="/index-page">Indices page</a> to see the different Indices in the project. To navigate through the available texts, use the <a href="/corpus-index">Corpus Index</a>. To navigate through the legal events, use the <a href="/event-index">Event Index</a>. To navigate through the people, use the <a href="/person-index">Person Index</a>. To navigate through the authors, used to <a href="/author-index">Author Index</a>. 

The site also contains dictionaries, created using <a href="https://ontoterminology.com/tedi">TEDI</a> (the ontoTerminology EDItor). These dictionaries are accessible through the <a href="/dictionaries-page">Dictionaries page</a>. The <a href="/processes-dictionary">Legal Processes dictionary</a> defines the legal processes in Classical Athens, the <a href="/bodies-dictionary">Legal Bodies dictionary</a> defines the government actors involved in legal processes, the <a href="/participants-dictionary">Legal Participants dictionary</a> defines the non-government actors involved in legal events, and the <a href="/oratory-dictionary">Oratory Types dictionary</a> defines the types of speeches in the corpus.

The Attic Oratory Database was created by Rachel Milio as a part of the PhD thesis "Semantic Annotation and Attic Oratory". This PhD is being completed as a part of the <a href="https://talos-ai4ssh.uoc.gr/">TALOS Project</a> at the University of Crete, supervised by Professor Christophe Roche, Dr. Maria Papadopoulou, and Professor Melina Tamiolaki.
