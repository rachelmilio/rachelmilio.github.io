<div><p>The Legal Bodies ontoterminology defines the types of legal bodies in Classical Athens relevant to the corpus of Attic Oratory. A legal body can be defined as "an individual or group who either proceeds over, judges, or is otherwise involved in a legal proceeding."</p>

<p>For more about ontoterminologies, visit the <a href="https://ontoterminology.com/">TEDI webpage</a>. An ontoterminology follows the Aristotelian notion of definition in which a concept is a unique combination of essential characteristics (i.e. a characteristic that if removed would result in a different concept). Ontoterminology also uses the hypothesis that a term designates a concept.</p>

<p>The Legal Bodies ontoterminology has terms in English and ancient Greek.</p>

<p>View the Legal Bodies dictionary <a href="/bodies-dictionary">here</a>.</p></div>

<div><ol><head>Essential characteristics are grouped along the following axes:</head>
<li>Judging jurisdiction: What legal processes is this body responsible for judging</li>
<li>Supervising jurisdiction: What legal processes is this body responsible for supervising</li>
<li>Other responsibilities: What is this body responsible for beyond judging and supervising</li>
<li>Size: How many people make up this body or hold this role</li>
<li>Selection: How the members are selcted for involvement</li>
<li>Membership: From what segment of the citizen body are members chosen</li>
<li>Term length: How long is this role held</li>
</ol></div>
