<div><p>The Legal Processes ontoterminology defines the types of legal processes in Classical Athens relevant to the corpus of Attic Oratory. A legal process can be defined as "a court dispute between at least two litigants for which a forensic speech would be written."</p>

<p>For more about ontoterminologies, visit the <a href="https://ontoterminology.com/">TEDI webpage</a>. An ontoterminology follows the Aristotelian notion of definition in which a concept is a unique combination of essential characteristics (i.e. a characteristic that if removed would result in a different concept). Ontoterminology also uses the hypothesis that a term designates a concept.</p>

<p>The Legal Processes ontoterminology has terms in English and ancient Greek.</p>

<p>View the Legal Processes dictionary <a href="/processes-dictionary">here</a>.</p></div>

<div><ol><head>Essential characteristics are grouped along the following axes:</head>
<li>Who are the litigants: Whether a process has a plaintiff and defendant, or multiple claimants</li>
<li>Who can bring forward a case: Whether a process is public or private</li>
<li>Whether the penalty is assessable</li>
<li>Motivation of the case</li>
<li>How the defendant is summoned to the trial</li>
<li>How the case is initiated</li>
<li>Which magistrate has jurisdiction over the case</li>
<li>Which legal body tries the case</li>
<li>What is the punishment if the defendant is convicted</li>
<li>What is the penalty for failed prosecution</li>
</ol></div>
