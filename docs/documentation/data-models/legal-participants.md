    <div><p>The Legal Participants ontoterminology defines the types of legal participants in Classical Athens relevant to the corpus of Attic Oratory. A legal participant can be defined as "an individual or group who either proceeds over, judges, or is otherwise involved in a legal proceeding."</p>

<p>For more about ontoterminologies, visit the <a href="https://ontoterminology.com/">TEDI webpage</a>. An ontoterminology follows the Aristotelian notion of definition in which a concept is a unique combination of essential characteristics (i.e. a characteristic that if removed would result in a different concept). Ontoterminology also uses the hypothesis that a term designates a concept.</p>

<p>The Legal Participants ontoterminology has terms in English and ancient Greek.</p>

<p>View the Legal Participants dictionary <a href="/participants-dictionary">here</a>.</p></div>

<div><ol><head>Essential characteristics are grouped along the following axes:</head>
<li>Level of interest: How involved is a participant in the outcome of the event</li>
<li>Role in proceeding: What is the role of the participant in an event</li>
<li>Status: What citizenship status does the participant have</li>
<li>Stakes: What are the consequences for a participant</li>
</ol></div>
